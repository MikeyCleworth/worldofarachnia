    • Old World Tarantulas

    Location

    Old world tarantulas are named as such due to originiating from countries in the pre-Columbus "Old World"; European, Asian and African tarantulas fall under the Old World category.

    Not for Beginners

    Old World tarantulas are only recommended for expert keepers. Their infamous temperaments combined with their lightning fast speed and potent venom bring a whole new challenge to tarantula husbandry. This isn't to say that new hobbyists can't get them, but prior research and confidence around spiders is imperative.

    Temperament and Risk

    Old World tarantulas often have demon-like temperaments. Furthermore, spiders in this collective pack a venom that's far more potent than their New World counterparts. As if this isn't enough, Old World tarantulas move quicker than the human eye can see, meaning they seem to "teleport" from one place to another. Their lightning-fast movements often takes people by surprise, resulting in accidents and injury to the keeper or the spider.

    The Defences of the Old World Tarantula

    Unlike New World species', Old World spiders lack urticating hairs. This leaves them with two options; flight or bite. Many Old Worlds are all too aware of their "medically significant" venom and will likely prefer to hold their ground and opt for choice number two. This is often bad news for the owner.

    Handling

    Handling is often discouraged in the tarantula community. Tarantulas are very delicate creatures and a fall from as low as three or four inches can easily result in a ruptured abdomen. Regardless of knowledge or handling experience, tarantulas are highly unpredictable and sudden movements can cause panic.

    When handling Old World species, the risks are far greater for both arachnid and keeper. Old World venom causes intense pain and can lead to hospitalisation.

    Summary

    Old World tarantualas are lightning-fast, typically highly aggressive and pack a painfully potent venom. Due to these three factors, they are only recommended for expert keepers. If you're looking at getting a pet tarantula, I would highly advise you to research a New World species.
